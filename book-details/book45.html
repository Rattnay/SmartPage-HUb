Why Investing is Important

Money loses value over time due to inflation—the gradual increase in prices of goods and services. If you keep all your money in a savings account, its purchasing power decreases over the years. Investing allows you to grow your wealth, outpace inflation, and build financial security for the future.

Key Benefits of Investing:

🔹 Beats Inflation – A typical savings account earns less than 1% in interest, while inflation averages around 2-3% per year. Investing can help your money grow faster than inflation.

🔹 Builds Wealth Over Time – Investing allows your money to work for you, compounding over time and leading to significant wealth accumulation.

🔹 Helps You Reach Financial Goals – Whether you’re saving for a house, education, retirement, or financial freedom, investing helps you reach your goals faster.

🔹 Creates Passive Income – Some investments, like dividend-paying stocks, real estate, and bonds, provide income without requiring active work.

🔹 Gives You Financial Security & Independence – Investing wisely can provide a financial cushion, helping you retire comfortably or pursue opportunities without financial stress.

A Simple Example: The Power of Compound Growth

Imagine you invest $5,000 per year at an 8% return (a common long-term stock market average).

  • After 10 years, you’d have $78,227.
  • After 20 years, it grows to $247,115.
  • After 30 years, it skyrockets to $611,729!

This is the power of compound interest—your money earns interest, and that interest earns more interest over time.
